### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Feet treading the yellow locust tree, a warrior most rare,
Talent is high but the road contested—appreciators of music are few.
One battle on the sandy field—no news returns,
Withered and worn in the western wind, the wild goose retreats in flight.

**Interpretation:**
Wisdom is broad but talent is sparse; advance an inch, retreat a foot. Wishing to rise to the azure clouds, but wings have not yet grown.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune describes someone with high ambitions but insufficient means to achieve them. Like a warrior seeking recognition but finding no appreciative audience, your talents may go unnoticed.
• Progress is extremely difficult—for every small step forward, you fall back. The conditions are not yet right for the success you seek.
• The wild goose retreating before the western wind symbolizes forced retreat. This is not the time to push forward; wait until you have developed the "wings" needed for flight.

### VIETNAMESE TRANSLATION

Hạ Quẻ (Cần cẩn thận)

**Bài Thơ Quẻ:**
Chân đạp hòe vàng, dũng sĩ kỳ,
Tài cao đường tranh, người thưởng thức hiếm.
Sa trường một trận không tin tức,
Héo úa gió tây nhạn lui bay.

**Giải Quẻ:**
Trí rộng tài thưa, tiến tấc lui thước. Muốn lên mây xanh, chưa mọc cánh bay.

**Ý Nghĩa Mở Rộng:**
• Quẻ này mô tả người có hoài bão cao nhưng thiếu phương tiện để đạt được. Như chiến binh tìm kiếm sự công nhận nhưng không tìm được khán giả đánh giá cao, tài năng của bạn có thể không được chú ý.
• Tiến bộ cực kỳ khó khăn—mỗi bước nhỏ tiến về phía trước, bạn lùi lại. Điều kiện chưa phù hợp cho thành công bạn tìm kiếm.
• Chim nhạn rút lui trước gió tây tượng trưng cho sự rút lui bắt buộc. Đây không phải lúc để đẩy mạnh; hãy chờ đợi cho đến khi bạn phát triển "đôi cánh" cần thiết để bay.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Upper Fortune (Good / Auspicious)

**Fortune Poem:**
Gain and loss, glory and decay—their source is in heaven,
Finding joy in heaven and knowing one's fate brings peace.
If you grasp this meaning and add your plans and hopes,
Gold and silk will fill your chests, goods will fill your ships.

**Interpretation:**
Movement and rest follow fate; gain and loss come from heaven. If you can follow and maintain this, blessings and fortune come naturally.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune teaches that accepting fate's role leads to greater prosperity than fighting against it. Those who understand heaven's way and align with it will find abundance.
• The key is attitude: "finding joy in heaven and knowing one's fate" creates the mental peace necessary for clear decision-making, which in turn leads to material success.
• Once you have this understanding, your plans and efforts will succeed naturally. Wealth, represented by gold, silk, and full ships, will come to those who maintain this balanced perspective.

• Một khi bạn có sự hiểu biết này, kế hoạch và nỗ lực của bạn sẽ thành công tự nhiên. Của cải, được đại diện bằng vàng, lụa, và thuyền đầy hàng, sẽ đến với những ai duy trì quan điểm cân bằng này.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Upper Fortune (Good / Auspicious)

**Fortune Poem:**
An arrow strikes—in the south, calls ring out, able to carry silk letters,
Then one knows the person is in a remote corner of Han.
Looking back, not yet meeting true news,
After the winter candles, before spring—just let it be for now.

**Interpretation:**
Divining messages not yet verified, only then will news be seen. Winter candles depart, spring comes—all matters sought will be auspicious.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune alludes to the famous story of Su Wu, who sent messages via wild geese while held captive. Like Su Wu who was eventually rescued, you will receive the news or help you've been waiting for.
• Timing is important: the transition from winter to spring (the period after winter candles/wax and before spring) is when positive developments will occur. Be patient until then.
• Messages, communications, and awaited news will arrive. Those who have been separated from loved ones or important information will find reconnection.

**Fortune Poem:**

Transforming into a dragon with horns, long already towering. Planning to head south for convenience, fitting to bid farewell to the vast sea.

• The reference to Zhuangzi's "Peng bird" rising on whirlwinds for a ten-thousand-mile journey indicates that grand ambitions are now achievable. You have completed your preparation period.
• Leave behind your current limitations (the "vast sea") and take flight toward your true destiny. Major transitions, relocations, or career changes are favored.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
Once I faced the rapids, envying the beautiful fish,
Wasting heart and effort on an empty pole.
Better to weave a net and go to the edge of the deep,
And naturally golden-scaled fish will meet your needs.

**Interpretation:**
Guarding a stump waiting for a rabbit, facing the deep envying fish—better to make changes, for there are other needs to meet.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune quotes the famous proverb: "Better to go home and weave a net than to stand by the pond longing for fish." Envying others' success without taking practical action wastes your energy.
• Stop wishing and start doing. Passive waiting (like "guarding a stump waiting for rabbits") will not bring results. Active preparation and proper tools are required for success.
• The "golden-scaled fish" represents your goals—they are achievable, but only through practical effort and the right approach. Change your methods to get real results.

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Demons and worries, for glory and nobility, are driven away,
But suddenly dense clouds lock up the great void.
Asking about the household's matters at their root,
Three souls and seven spirits enter the land of Huaxu.

**Interpretation:**
There is something haunting in the home; matters divined cannot be planned. Tongues are dark and confused; illness divined has not recovered.

• This fortune indicates spiritual or supernatural disturbances affecting the household. "Three souls and seven spirits" entering Huaxu (the dream realm) suggests someone's vitality or consciousness is compromised.
• Dark forces or negative influences are blocking progress and clarity. The "dense clouds locking the void" represents obscured judgment and blocked pathways.
• Health matters, especially those affecting consciousness or vitality, are concerning. Seek both practical medical help and spiritual cleansing if problems persist.
