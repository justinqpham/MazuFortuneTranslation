### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
Having crossed over layered mountains, waves recede behind,
This body now rejoices, emerging from the dust.
One clap of thunder shakes the nine heavens,
Rising straight up to the clouds, proclaiming fame aloud.

**Interpretation:**
The hexagram's main matter: first difficult, then easy. One thunderclap, and heavenly decree takes flight.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune describes a breakthrough after prolonged struggle. Like emerging from dust and crossing mountains, your difficulties are giving way to clarity and success.
• The thunder imagery signifies sudden, dramatic change for the better. What seemed impossible will shift quickly once the right moment arrives.
• Patience through hardship leads to recognition. Your efforts will eventually be noticed and celebrated, bringing you honor and advancement.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Feng Yi drums up waves, washing becomes disaster,
The broken cable drags the boat away, never to return.
Even if divine beings secretly provide protection,
You yourself may escape harm, but wealth will still be lost.

**Interpretation:**
When disaster wishes to come, how can one escape? If the body is not harmed, wealth must still be lost.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune warns of unavoidable difficulties ahead. Like a boat with a broken cable being swept away by waves, some losses cannot be prevented no matter how hard you try.
• Feng Yi is the god of rivers and floods in Chinese mythology; his appearance suggests turbulent circumstances beyond your control that will bring disruption to your plans.
• The best outcome here is minimizing damage: you may preserve your health and safety, but expect financial losses. Accept this and do not fight against forces too great to overcome.

• The reference to "rice and grain" reminds us that sustenance and stability matter more than ambitious overreaching. Be content with sufficiency rather than constantly striving for more.
• Avoid greed and avoid depending on powerful connections that may not always be reliable. Self-sufficiency and good timing are your best strategies now.

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Official courts and private gates—how strange indeed!
Two people meet and naturally suspect and guess at each other.

**Interpretation:**
Official matters involve emptiness; disaster arises from within the walls. Spring and summer bring delays; autumn and winter bring good fortune.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune warns of deception and hidden enemies, particularly in official or business dealings. People you meet may harbor ulterior motives—trust cautiously.
• The phrase "disaster arises from within the walls" (禍起蕭牆) warns that threats come from those close to you, not strangers. Beware of betrayal from within your own circle.
• The timing advice is clear: spring and summer bring difficulties and delays, but conditions improve in autumn and winter. Postpone important decisions if possible.
