### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Upper Fortune (Good / Auspicious)

**Fortune Poem:**
A guest comes asking how your child fares,
Fields can be plowed and land can be hoed,
After a year of tilling comes success,
Better in late autumn or early winter.

**Interpretation:**
A guest comes with inquiries, all matters are not in vain, if asking about pregnancy expect a boy, only through diligence comes achievement.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune speaks of agricultural metaphors for life's endeavors - just as fields require patient cultivation, your current projects need steady, consistent effort before yielding results.
• The timing advice suggests that patience through the year will bring harvest in late autumn or early winter, indicating delayed but certain success for those who persist.
• Questions about family matters, particularly children or new ventures, receive favorable indications - diligent work will be rewarded.

Upper Fortune (Good / Auspicious)

**Fortune Poem:**
Joy strikes between the brows, a spot of yellow,
From now on letters urge the son to court,
The imperial countenance brings glory and honor,
Daily waiting to be graced with royal light.

**Interpretation:**
Joy strikes between the brows, the future path leads to prosperity, seeking office brings prominence, marriage will surely succeed.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• A yellow spot between the brows is a traditional Chinese omen of impending good fortune and honor - this stick predicts recognition and advancement in official or career matters.
• The imagery of being called to imperial court suggests that authority figures will notice and reward your efforts; promotions, honors, or important recognition are forthcoming.
• Marriage prospects are especially favorable - unions formed during this time will be blessed with harmony and mutual respect.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
In the game of Go, what determines win or loss?
In the end, return to him a full board,
Who would waste true heart and bitter effort,
Better to seek peace early on.

**Interpretation:**
The weak cannot match the strong, facing matters is difficult, the few cannot defeat the many, seeking harmony is best.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune uses the strategic game of Go as a metaphor - sometimes the wisest move is not to fight but to recognize when the odds are against you and seek compromise instead.
• Rather than exhausting yourself in struggles where success is uncertain, conserve your energy and resources by finding middle ground with opponents or obstacles.
• The advice is pragmatic: know your limitations, avoid unnecessary conflicts, and choose diplomatic solutions over confrontational ones.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Father parts from son, wife leaves husband,
Like beans boiling beans, kin grow distant,
Deep in the night the bright moon fades at dawn,
Who knew distant trees hold crying birds.

**Interpretation:**
Flesh and blood separate, the six relations are not harmonious, arguments and quarrels arise, also beware of livestock matters.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune warns of family discord and separation - relationships that should be close may become strained, with relatives turning against each other like the famous metaphor of beans boiling beans (brothers harming brothers).
• The imagery of fading moonlight and distant crying birds suggests loneliness and sorrow; important bonds may weaken or break during this period, requiring careful attention to maintain.
• Exercise extreme caution in family matters and property disputes; avoid escalating conflicts and work toward reconciliation where possible.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
The Vermilion Bird arrives, matters are not good,
Changing plans and taking action invites much suspicion,
Illness entangles the stomach, how to avoid it,
Arguments arise and wealth is lost.

**Interpretation:**
The Vermilion Bird arrives at your person, actions bring no benefit, illness has not retreated, arguments and quarrels persist.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• In Chinese astrology, the Vermilion Bird (Zhu Que) appearing signifies a period of difficulties, particularly involving disputes, gossip, and legal troubles - be extra cautious in all dealings.
• Health concerns, especially digestive issues, are highlighted; this is not the time to neglect physical wellbeing or make major changes to your routine.
• Financial losses through arguments, lawsuits, or poor decisions are likely; avoid risky investments and heated disputes that could drain your resources.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
On the plains the rabbit dies, the dog faces cooking,
When birds are gone the bow is stored, affairs don't prosper,
When merit is full and success complete, one must step back,
Otherwise disaster and calamity will come upon you.

**Interpretation:**
Do not climb to high places, do not tread in dangerous places, if you can step back, only then can you avoid worry.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune references the famous Chinese proverb about the hunting dog being cooked after the rabbit is caught - it warns about the dangers of outliving your usefulness to those in power.
• The wisdom here is about knowing when to retreat: once you've achieved success or completed your mission, it's time to step back gracefully rather than push for more, which invites downfall.
• Ambition beyond your current achievement is dangerous now; consolidate what you have, avoid overreaching, and recognize that strategic withdrawal is sometimes the path to preservation.

• The message is clear: consolidate your gains, don't overextend, and remember that a cup filled too full will spill - moderation and humility protect long-term success.
