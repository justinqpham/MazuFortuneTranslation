### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
People say there are immortals on the sea,
But how does one go there and not return?
In this world there is no path to the mysterious gate,
The alchemist's snow flower—why is there no real trace?

**Interpretation:**
Matters divined are not yet accomplished; hopes have not yet arrived. Generally, it is empty flowers, with no real meaning inside.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune warns against chasing illusions. Like the myth of immortals on the sea, some goals you pursue may be beautiful fantasies rather than achievable realities.
• The "mysterious gate" to success or enlightenment has no worldly path—what you seek may not be attainable through ordinary means.
• Current plans lack substance. Like alchemical transformations that leave no real trace, your current endeavors may appear promising but contain no genuine value. Reassess whether your goals are realistic.

### VIETNAMESE TRANSLATION

Trung Quẻ (Bình thường / Trung bình)

**Bài Thơ Quẻ:**
Người nói biển có thần tiên,
Đi sao chưa về được?
Thế gian không đường đến huyền quan,
Hoa tuyết phương sĩ sao không dấu thực?

**Giải Quẻ:**
Bói việc chưa thành, mong việc chưa đến. Đại để hoa hư, trong không có thực ý.

**Ý Nghĩa Mở Rộng:**
• Quẻ này cảnh báo về việc đuổi theo ảo tưởng. Như huyền thoại về thần tiên trên biển, một số mục tiêu bạn theo đuổi có thể là những giấc mơ đẹp chứ không phải thực tế có thể đạt được.
• "Huyền quan" đến thành công hoặc giác ngộ không có con đường trần thế—những gì bạn tìm kiếm có thể không đạt được bằng phương tiện thông thường.
• Các kế hoạch hiện tại thiếu thực chất. Như những biến đổi thuật kim đan không để lại dấu vết thực, những nỗ lực hiện tại của bạn có thể có vẻ hứa hẹn nhưng không chứa giá trị thực sự. Hãy đánh giá lại xem mục tiêu của bạn có thực tế không.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Upper Fortune (Good / Auspicious)

**Fortune Poem:**
One crane can steal from the many pond birds in waves,
The flood dragon ultimately does not use—great fortune.
The Marquis of Lu had not yet met Mencius,
How could the Zang clan obstruct him?

**Interpretation:**
Auspicious people do good; blessings come from being a guest elsewhere. Above the pit flows water; there is no obstruction.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune uses classical references to indicate that nothing can obstruct your destined success. Just as Mencius eventually met his patron despite the Zang clan's opposition, obstacles to your advancement will fail.
• You stand out from the crowd like a crane among ordinary birds. Your exceptional qualities will be recognized and rewarded, regardless of what others try to do.
• Water flows without obstruction—your path forward is clear. Act with confidence, knowing that opposition will not succeed in blocking your progress.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
Thunder and rain pour down at the time of resolution,
All who encounter this difficulty will have it removed.
Ten thousand matters are difficult to release forever,
Seeking wealth—if not yet, it delays.

**Interpretation:**
Do not believe glib words; thunder and rain bring wetness. All matters will eventually be released; not favorable for seeking wealth.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune speaks of a time of clearing and resolution, like a cleansing thunderstorm. Problems and difficulties you face will eventually be washed away, but the process takes time.
• Be wary of smooth talkers ("glib words"). Not everything you hear can be trusted during this turbulent period. Wait for clarity before making decisions.
• Financial matters face delays—this is not an auspicious time for seeking wealth or making investments. Other matters may resolve themselves, but money concerns will lag behind.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
A dog's bark—how can it harm the brightness of sun and moon?
Iron harm, the bit—people's lips and tongue-swords all become empty.
The dog—why harm itself, in the end without benefit?
Coming to eat one's own body.

**Interpretation:**
Slanderous words are not yet heard; do not believe glib tongues. Those who poison others are not yet poisoned; heaven's principle will naturally become clear.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune addresses slander and malicious speech. Like a dog barking at the sun and moon, those who speak against you cannot truly dim your brightness—their attacks are futile.
• However, the warning is also about self-destruction: those who engage in harmful speech and actions ultimately harm themselves. "Eating one's own body" represents the self-destructive nature of malice.
• Do not engage with slanderers or respond to baseless accusations. Heaven's justice will prevail, and the truth will become clear without your intervention.

Sắt hại hàm thiếc, người môi lưỡi kiếm đều thành không.

• Đừng tham gia với những kẻ vu khống hoặc phản hồi các cáo buộc vô căn cứ. Công lý của trời sẽ thắng, và sự thật sẽ trở nên rõ ràng mà không cần bạn can thiệp.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Middle Fortune (Neutral / Mixed)

**Fortune Poem:**
Lost things withdraw compensation—quietly without feeling,
Searching south, seeking north—bitterly exhausting the heart.
You know the person went east and west,
But waters are wide, depths are deep—where to search?

**Interpretation:**
Hoped-for matters have not arrived; east, south, west, north. Lost items are not yet recovered; vainly exhausting heart and strength.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune specifically addresses lost things—whether physical objects, people, or opportunities—and indicates they will be very difficult to recover. Searching in every direction will prove fruitless.
• The imagery of wide waters and deep depths suggests that what is lost has gone beyond your reach. Accept this rather than exhausting yourself in futile searching.
• More broadly, some things you hope for will not arrive. This is a time to accept losses rather than continue pursuing what cannot be recovered.

### ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Lower Fortune (Caution Advised)

**Fortune Poem:**
Rat's teeth, sparrow's claws—secretly spying on each other,
Summer passes, praying for cold—resentment and sorrow arise.
The Chu kingdom's things need not cause suspicion over loss,
The King of Chu loses, outside.

**Interpretation:**
Slanderous words arise from all quarters; moreover, guard yourself. Matters are not fully spoken; disaster arises from within the walls.

**Extended Interpretation:**
• This fortune warns of enemies working secretly against you. The imagery of rats and sparrows spying represents petty people scheming in the shadows.
• The classical reference to the King of Chu reminds us that suspicion and accusations among one's own people cause more harm than external threats. The famous phrase "disaster arising from within the walls" indicates betrayal from those close to you.
• Guard yourself against slander and be careful what you say—not everything should be fully disclosed. Trust cautiously, as threats may come from unexpected quarters within your own circle.
